Transportation

The increasing demands of the transportation industry have set in motion technological development in the sector, with computer vision at its center.

From autonomous vehicles to parking occupancy detection, the Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) has become a critical field for promoting the efficiency, effectiveness, and safety of transportation.

Transport is a part of our daily lives, so computer science's contributions to this industry make a hugely positive contribution to society...

Self driving cars

In 2022 autonomous vehicles are no longer science fiction.

Currently, thousands of engineers and software developers worldwide are already testing and improving the reliability and safety of self-driving cars.

Computer vision is used to detect and classify objects (e.g., road signs or traffic lights), create 3D maps or motion estimation, and plays a key role in making science fiction reality.


Self-driving cars collect data on their surroundings from sensors and cameras, process and interpret it, and respond accordingly.

Researches working on the ADAS technology combine techniques such as pattern recognition, feature extraction, object tracking, and 3D vision to develop real-time algorithms that assist driving activity.

Pedestrian detection

Pedestrian tracking and detection have become a vital computer vision research area because of its potential impact on the design of pedestrian protection systems and smart cities.

It uses cameras to identify and locate pedestrians in images or videos while considering variations related to body position, clothing, occlusion, lighting in different scenarios, and background clutter.


The detection of pedestrians has applications in fields such as autonomous driving, traffic management, and transit safety and efficiency.

Conclusion

The transport sector will benefit greatly from computer science. Specifically the use of computer vision for autonomous cars and detection of road signs or pedestrians is almost essential for future driving technology.
